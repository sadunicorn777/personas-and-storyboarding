The interface I chose is a BROWN UNIVERSITY RESIDENCE HALL ELEVATOR.

â› Interface Diagram

Most of the residence hall elevators at Brown University share the same template of characteristics, which are the ones considered in this project. These are an exterior panel to call the elevator and specify a direction of travel, an interior bar that may be gripped, and an interior panel of buttons to either specify a desired destination or request emergency assistance. Most elevators also include an interior ID recognition device which affords the user access to the buttons. Some do not; an example is the Minden Hall elevator.

The residence hall elevator is trying to solve the problem of long and/or arduous ascension or descension processes for users who are commuting from the building entrance to their place of residence, or vice versa.

Please tell me about your purpose for using this elevator. Go into as much detail as you want.

User A's purpose for using the elevator is to travel eight floors to her residence in Minden Hall. Being on the eighth floor, taking the stairs is unfathomable to her.

User B uses the Wellness Center elevator the most frequently for the purpose of visiting friends; he is not a resident of the Wellness Center which affects his elevator experience.

Patterns: The elevator is used to facilitate socialization.

How often do you use the elevator? Why this frequency?

User A typically uses the elevator every day because her schedule involves mobility out of her residence, and the stairs would be unnecessarily exhausting: a hindrance to her psyche.

User B uses the elevator at least 2-3 times a week because many of his friends live in the Wellness Center.

User C uses the elevator very infrequently. In the elevator, she doesn't feel in control of her own pace so she takes the stairs. Furthermore, the stairs are closer to the entrance and closer to her place of residence (elevator's proximity is too far).

User A expects to be transported to her destination. She expects that the interior buttons will work which makes it extra annoying when they don't.

Patterns: The size of the elevator may pose an obstacle, users are motivated to avoid cramming. Users are motivated to preoccupy themselves (contemplation, phone, reading surroundings)
